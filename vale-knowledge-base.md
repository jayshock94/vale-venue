## The Space

### Total Size
6,500 square feet total. That includes the main hall, two guest suites, and the caterer's kitchen.

### Main Hall
4,500 square feet. Open, blank canvas. Clients create their own layout. The space has been used for plated dinners, dance floors, ceremonies, receptions, cocktail hours, trade shows, and exercise classes.

### Guest Suites
Two guest suites for the guest(s) of honor. Guests consistently mention how large and spacious they are.

### Kitchen
Commercial caterer's kitchen. Includes a three-compartment sink, commercial fridge and freezer, and stainless steel prep tables. Caterers have specifically praised how spacious it is.

### Outdoor Space
Just outside the south double doors of the main hall there is a 61ft x 74ft area (over 4,500 sq ft). It is currently a parking lot, not a traditional courtyard (yet). Clients have used it for ceremonies, cocktail hours, luncheons, pre-party gatherings, and food truck setups. It is conveniently located for food truck access.

### Parking
Over 200 parking spots on site, privately owned by The Vale. This is one of the venue's biggest selling points. It means:
- Guests are not walking blocks on busy roads to reach the event
- Vendors doing delivery, drop-off, and pickup are less likely to charge extra fees (many vendors charge more when venue access is difficult)
- The lot has its own private drop-off and pickup area
- Clients have also used the lot as an additional 4,500 sq ft of celebration space

## Pricing

### Range
Events range from $1,500 to $4,000.

### What Affects the Price
- How long they are using the space (a few hours vs. all day)
- Whether they are using just the main hall, or also the kitchen and guest suites
- Whether they are reserving the side lot
- Whether alcohol will be served (requires security guards, licensed bartender, and additional insurance)
- Whether they need tables and chairs, and which type
- Whether they need a day-of coordinator
- Whether they need decor setup
- Whether they need setup and teardown

### The Pricing Dilemma (Internal Context)
Bobbi's biggest frustration: the $1,500 starting price attracts budget clients with luxury expectations. Clients willing to pay $5,000 see the low starting price and assume the venue is not for them. Clients looking for a deal see the number and expect a $5,000 experience at $1,500. Current marketing targets the ideal $5,000 client, but both tiers matter to the business.

Bobbi has considered an a la carte / package builder approach. Let clients add items to a cart, build their own package, see the total, and submit it. The Vale then reviews, confirms availability and feasibility, and sends a contract.

## Time Blocks

- Standard rental is a 12-hour block: 9am to 9pm, or 10am to 10pm
- Additional hours available at $300 to $500 per hour, depending on the date
- Time blocks can be adjusted if nothing else is booked (for example, 8am to 8pm or 11am to 11pm)
- Early setup the night before is possible if nothing is already booked, for a flat fee of $500
- Weekend nights often have back-to-back events (a reception ending at 8 or 9pm followed by a dance party from 9 or 10pm until 12 or 1am)

## Catering Policy

**Open catering. No preferred vendor list. No markup.**

Clients can bring any caterer they want, or bring their own food. The Vale has hosted cultural events requiring homemade dishes that no outside caterer could prepare. They honor the uniqueness of every event.

### Insurance Requirement
If the client is not using a licensed and insured caterer (one that carries their own insurance or food handler's permit), the client needs to get event insurance. There is no additional charge from The Vale for this.

### Food Trucks
Welcome and encouraged. The outdoor space off the south double doors is conveniently set up for food truck access.

### Vendor Recommendations
The Vale is happy to recommend caterers or other vendors but does not maintain a preferred vendor list. No fees for using outside vendors. This saves clients money and gives them full flexibility.

## Alcohol Policy

Alcohol is allowed. Requirements:
- Licensed bartender (required by Utah state law)
- Security guards (required by Utah state law)
- Additional insurance (The Vale's premiums are higher because they allow alcohol)
- All of the above are at additional cost to the client

## Booking Process

### How People Find The Vale
- Website
- Facebook Marketplace
- Word of mouth and referrals
- Instagram and social media DMs

### Most Common Contact Methods
Text, phone call, or Facebook Messenger.

### First Questions People Ask
1. Are you available on [date]?
2. How much does it cost?

### From Inquiry to Booking
1. Client reaches out (text, call, form, DM)
2. Bobbi sends them a link to an inquiry form, which feeds into their CRM (HoneyBook)
3. Bobbi contacts them by call, email, or text to discuss fit
4. If it looks like a match, they schedule an in-person tour
5. After the tour, Bobbi sends a proposal (basically a contract and invoice, negotiable)
6. If they want to book, they approve the proposal
7. Contract and invoice are sent via HoneyBook
8. Client signs the contract and pays a 50% retainer
9. Remaining balance is split into monthly payments, due in full 30 days before the event
10. If the event is less than 30 days away, full payment is due at signing

### Post-Booking
- Two complimentary walk-throughs after booking (for the client, their wedding planner, decorator, etc. to see the space and take measurements)
- Additional walk-throughs beyond two are charged
- No tours or walk-throughs happen while another client's event is in progress
- Setup and decoration happen on the day of the event, or earlier if arranged and paid for

### Cancellation Policy
- No refunds
- One reschedule allowed, contingent on venue availability

### Availability Checking
Currently done by text, email, or phone call. No calendar on the website. Bobbi has explained why: a single day can include a morning photoshoot, a daytime reception, and a late-night dance party, so a simple open/closed calendar does not reflect real availability. Human involvement is needed for accurate booking.

## Ideal Client

The DIY client who wants a blank space to create her own event. Someone who sees the value in an open venue at a price point that leaves room in her budget to hire the decorator, florist, and caterer she actually wants. Someone with taste and a plan, not someone looking for the cheapest option and expecting luxury included.

---

## What Closes the Deal

- Flexibility. The Vale works with clients on time blocks, vendor choices, and layout.
- Location and parking. Easy freeway access, quiet setting, 200+ private spots.
- The versatility of the space itself.

---

## What Loses the Deal

- The blank canvas is intimidating for some clients. They want to walk into a space that already looks finished, not one they have to decorate.
- Some people see it as too much work to find their own vendors and handle their own setup.

---

## What Clients Struggle With

Logistics. Making sure the venue, the date, the time block, the caterer, the photographer, the decorator, the day-of coordinator, and the wedding planner all line up. Coordinating all of those moving pieces is what stresses people out.
